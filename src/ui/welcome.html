Veil

What Veil Does

Veil automatically repairs websites to make them more accessible. It runs on every page you visit. It fixes missing image descriptions, labels unlabeled buttons, repairs heading structure, dismisses cookie popups, detects CAPTCHAs, and more.

You don't need to do anything — it works automatically.

How to Use Voice Mode

Click the Veil icon in your browser toolbar. That's it. One click starts voice mode. Click again to stop. Your screen reader will announce the icon.

Once voice mode is on, just talk naturally:

  • "What's on this page?" — hear a page summary
  • "Tell me about admissions" — find and navigate to that topic
  • "Take me to [section name]" — scroll to a section and read it
  • "Click" followed by a button or link name
  • "Scroll down" or "scroll up"
  • "Next heading", "next button", or "next link"
  • "Go back" or "go forward"
  • "What am I missing?" — accessibility gap report
  • "Stop" — stop speaking
  • "Help" — hear all available commands

You can also right-click the Veil icon for quick options: Page Summary, What Am I Missing, and Settings.

How It Works

1. Tier 1 — Instant Repair. Runs in under 50 milliseconds on every page. Fixes images, buttons, form labels, headings, landmarks, and focus traps. No internet needed.
2. Tier 2 — Smart Repair. Automatically dismisses cookie consent banners. Detects CAPTCHAs and tells you if there's an audio alternative. Announces page changes on single-page apps.
3. Tier 3 — Vision AI. Optional. If you add an API key in settings, Veil can capture a screenshot and describe what's visually on the page. This is the "What Am I Missing?" report.

Privacy

Veil collects no data. No analytics. No tracking. If you add an API key for Tier 3, page content is sent to your chosen provider only. Your API key never leaves your device.

That's everything. Veil is now running on every website you visit. Close this tab and go to any website. Click the Veil icon in your toolbar to start talking.
